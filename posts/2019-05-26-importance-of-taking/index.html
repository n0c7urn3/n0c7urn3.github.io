Importance of Taking Care of One’s Health

2019-05-26
#sick  #doctors  #self-care 

Yay, first “Personal” category post!

So, I’m definitely not “cool” enough to have people tracking my every move (except for my assigned FBI agent. Hi!), but if you look at my #100daysofcode tracker, you might notice only a single tally mark since the original publication, and a total of “1.5.” How could I have completed 1.5 days of code?

Well, I got sick. It’s just a cold, but colds knock me out pretty bad, and they’re viruses, so not much can be done about them outside of fluids/rest (technically, I suppose antibiotics could treat them, but I’m not contributing to the overuse of antibiotics). Tuesday morning (my Monday evening, ‘cause overnight shift), I was feeling pretty crummy, but sat down to do more freeCodeCamp, and got about 30 minutes in and decided I needed to be horizontal, so I updated the tracker and went to bed.

When I woke up in the evening, I felt even worse and called out of work. The last time I got sick, I was a contractor, so I wasn’t afforded any PTO. I had little choice but to come in at that time. I wore a facemask to try to contain the germs, and was drinking Theraflu and eating cough drops for every shift for the week I was sick. This time, I’m fortunate enough to be a full time employee and have the option to call out without worrying about how I’m going to make rent, but it was still a debate because I was stuck in that mindset of “I can’t afford to call out for this.”

I made the right call. Since Tuesday, I’ve been home, spending my time alternating between sleeping and taking as much cold medicine as is safe (always go by the recommended dosages kids). It has meant I haven’t been able to make it to the gym (I’ve got about 30lb minimum I want to get rid of), or work on the #100daysofcode challenge, or work on my CySA+ (honestly, I’m about ready for that, I think), or cook (pho delivery for the win!). This has kind of put my life on hold for the last week, which got me thinking.

Outside of ER/urgent care visits, I haven’t actually seen a doctor since around the time I turned 18. That’s almost 11 years. No physicals, no screenings, nothing. ER has been for the flu, and urgent care was just because I needed a doctor’s note for work (that was $200 piece of paper, by the way). My cat has been to the vet for a checkup more recently than I have.

So, I’ve done some research. My girlfriend really likes her provider group, and they’re in network for me, so I plan to reach out on second-Monday (since it was pointed out to me that Monday is Memorial Day, Tuesday is now second-Monday). I think that every step in a positive/self-care direction is supposed to be scary, and this is no different. I’m excited and apprehensive, and plan to make a follow up once I have an appointment (light details, ‘cause personal info). To my readers, take care of yourself, if you haven’t been to doctor in a while and can afford it, make an appointment, at least get a physical.
